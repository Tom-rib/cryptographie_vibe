Hash: 35b95f7c0f63631c453220fb2a86f
Mask: ?u?u?l?l?u?u?s

Command used:
  hashcat -m 0 -a 3 35b95f7c0f63631c453220fb2a86f '?u?u?l?l?u?u?s'

Expected result format:
  Message: [decoded password from hash]
  Time to crack: [seconds]

Note: The mask ?u?u?l?l?u?u?s requires testing ~10 billion combinations:
  - 2 uppercase letters: 26^2 = 676
  - 2 lowercase letters: 26^2 = 676
  - 2 uppercase letters: 26^2 = 676
  - 1 special character: ~32
  Total: 676 × 676 × 676 × 32 ≈ 10 billion combinations

At 1.7M attempts/second (CPU), this would take ~2-3 minutes.
With GPU acceleration (hashcat): seconds to minutes.

This demonstrates why MD5 is insecure:
- Even with the limited mask, 10 billion combinations are tested quickly
- Modern hashing like bcrypt with salt makes such attacks infeasible
